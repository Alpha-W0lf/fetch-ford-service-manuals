- **Operator outcome:** Capture the important families during the subscription window while preserving useful retries and avoiding prolonged worker-slot or wall-clock waste from persistent failures, auth issues, or retry storms.

## Phase 2 — Historical context and runtime evidence (2026-07-10)

- **Cross-family 403 cluster is shared, not model-specific.** Failed F-350/F-450/F-550, Navigator, Expedition, and later E-Series jobs all show the same early `TreeAndCover/workshop` POST returning `HTTP 403 Forbidden` / `Ford CDN returned Access Denied`. Representative logs: `logs/2016-f-350.log`, `logs/2012-f-450.log`, `logs/2022-f-550.log`, `logs/2007-navigator.log`, `logs/2008-expedition.log`.
- **This is not evidence that the subscription ended.** Successful later vehicles (`2020-expedition`, `2013-f-350`, `2012-f-550`, `2013-f-550`, Explorers) and the live PTS/CDP cookie refresh demonstrate that the service recovered. The correct classification is a transient shared content-auth/CDN-access failure until a failing request can be reproduced against a freshly verified session.
- **The retry cost is real and severe.** `capture-gaps.json` records large repeated 403 batches: `manuals/2014-f-550/capture-gaps.json` shows workshop auth gaps with 14 attempts; `manuals/2017-navigator/capture-gaps.json` shows repeated workshop 403s with 52 attempts. The current stale-gap threshold only affects `incomplete`; `failed` vehicles remain queueable after they have at least 50 PDFs (`scripts/queue-lib.js`).
- **Not all events are auth.** `logs/2022-f-450.log` records a real `net::ERR_INTERNET_DISCONNECTED`, later retry, and a 10-minute CDP-lock timeout. These require separate failure taxonomy and retry policy; treating every failure as subscription expiry would be wrong.
- **Existing controls and their limit:** `runOne` records auth only on the final `failed` path (`lib/bulk-orchestrator-lib.js:565–585`) and the global circuit breaker pauses dispatch after the threshold. It does not classify/limit repeated partial-download failure at the vehicle level. Guide 04.3 covers the distinct INCOMPLETE-auth early-return path and its missing gap accounting.
- **Reusable patterns verified:** Guide 04.2 already provides PID/log-stale reap and disk-truth status repair; `scripts/queue-lib.js` provides `isStaleIncomplete` / attempt-based deprioritization; `lib/bulk-auth-log.js` supplies the current broad auth detector. These are extension points, not reasons to add a second scheduler.

## Phase 3 — Architecture and standards alignment (2026-07-10)

- **Architecture contract:** `docs/reference/architecture.md` separates headless workshop/wiring access, live-Chrome connector access, and Node fleet orchestration. A fix must preserve that separation and must not place retry policy in `bulk-download.sh`.
- **Existing semantics:** `src/httpRetry.ts` deliberately retries transient network/5xx conditions but not HTTP 403; 403 is instead classified as `auth` by `src/captureGaps.ts` and `src/ptsAuth.ts`. This behavior is sound at request scope, but does not supply a bounded *vehicle-level* policy after many partial 403s.
- **Observed contract gap:** `CaptureGaps.record()` correctly increments persistent per-artifact attempt counts, yet `scripts/queue-lib.js` only deprioritizes stale `incomplete` records. Partial `failed` jobs with >=50 PDFs remain eligible and are retried without a comparable reason-aware cooldown or escalation policy.
- **Applicable standards:** `AGENTS.md` requires the smallest correct change, existing Node orchestration libraries, one active bulk run, and no refactor during subscription bulk. `prompt_work_session_standards.md` requires explicit scope, source-specific error handling, deterministic tests, and documented blast radius. Observability guidance supports structured, reason-coded events but does not justify an unrelated logging framework during the active run.
- **Initial boundary decision:** Keep Guide 04.3 limited to its verified fast INCOMPLETE auth storm and missing connectors-only gap accounting. Create a separate 04.4 track for *reason-aware partial-failure policy* across the `failed` and stale-partial paths, including network/CDP distinctions and family retry triage. This avoids coupling two independently testable state transitions.

## Phase 4 — Verification and gap analysis (2026-07-10)

- **Verified live access:** At the investigation start, live PTS Chrome was connected on `:9222`; cookie refresh succeeded and active Explorer downloads continued. The family-wide 403 cluster therefore cannot be labeled a confirmed subscription expiry.
- **Verified recovery evidence:** Later success in the same restart (`2020-expedition`, `2013-f-350`, `2012-f-550`, `2013-f-550`, and multiple Explorers) proves that both the family catalog and PTS access can work after the failure windows.
- **Verified waste mechanism:** Individual 403 gaps are persisted with increasing attempts (`CaptureGaps.record()`), but long partial jobs can still return `failed`; `isQueued()` treats them as retryable once 50 PDFs exist, while stale-attempt deprioritization applies only to `incomplete`. This creates repeatable wall-clock spend after a broad 403 burst.
- **KNOWN:** The global auth circuit breaker uses a rolling 15-minute stamp window (`lib/bulk-circuit-breaker.js`) and refreshes cookies after its threshold; it protects the fleet but is too coarse to prevent repeated expensive retries of a single partially captured vehicle after the global service recovers.
- **UNKNOWN (not a blocker for planning):** The external cause of each 403 (stale content entitlement, Akamai policy, or Ford CDN behavior) cannot be distinguished from current evidence. The implementation must be cause-agnostic: use observed error class and recovery evidence, not an assertion that the subscription is expired.
- **UNKNOWN (Phase 0 reproduction):** Whether 2014/2015 Super Duty and Explorer gaps reproduce after a freshly healthy PTS session. A controlled, one-vehicle retry after bulk stop must determine whether they are recoverable auth gaps or durable source/content defects.
- **Readiness:** A context summary and guide may be drafted with a Phase 0 evidence-reproduction gate. No code should be implemented while the bulk run is active.
- **Verified worker-level root cause:** `src/workshop/saveEntireManual.ts` records each 403 as an `auth` gap and continues. After five consecutive auth failures it only reloads the already-exported cookie file, resets the streak, and continues iterating (`maybeRefreshCookiesOnAuthStreak`, lines 23–46; catch paths at lines 90–107 and 162–188). This explains why one vehicle can accumulate dozens of identical 403 gaps (14 or 52 attempts) even though request-level retry correctly refuses to retry HTTP 403. A reason-aware in-worker stop is required; an orchestrator-only cooldown happens too late to prevent that wasted work.
- **Independent cross-check refined the taxonomy:** zero-PDF `TreeAndCover` 403 failures are stranded because `scripts/queue-lib.js:isQueued()` requeues `failed` only at >=50 PDFs; meanwhile partial jobs can fail at wiring TOC when `src/wiring/fetchTableOfContents.ts` throws a 403 without a structured gap, making the orchestrator classify them as retryable `failed`. Guide 04.4 must cover both recovery of verified-auth zero-PDF failures and reason-preserving wiring-TOC failure handling, in addition to worker-level partial auth stops.
- **Live-process correction at 11:27:** `ps` confirmed `bulk-orchestrator.js` PID 33924, its `caffeinate`/`bulk-download.sh` supervisor, and two active `yarn start` workers (`2022-explorer`, `2015-mustang`). A stale-log/lock conclusion must not override current process evidence; the run remains active and implementation stays deferred.

This reinforces the 04.4 need to record a blocking early auth gap and intentionally route it to `incomplete`, rather than altering the global failed-job threshold.

Keep the staged approach: refine and validate 04.3 first; then perform the controlled 04.4 reproduction matrix before creating its implementation guide.
